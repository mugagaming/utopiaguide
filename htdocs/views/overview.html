Basic Geography

Utopia is a world of vast oceans dotted with Islands, and on each of these islands are several Kingdoms. Each Kingdom has up to 25 provinces, one of which will be yours to rule over. Together with the rest of your Kingdom, you will play a vital role in war, growth and diplomacy. Govern your peasantry, army, wizards, and thieves - and aim to conquer the world.

Time

In the realm of Utopia, time moves much faster than it does on Earth. That which you know as an "hour", is a full Utopian Day, and an Earth day is an entire Utopian Month. And yes, this means there are 24 days in a month. Each Utopian Year contains seven months. Know that your advisors do not know of Earth time and will only speak of Utopian time.

At the beginning of every Age everyone starts anew with a fledgling province, giving each ruler and each kingdom the opportunity to take the ranks by storm. Each Age lasts about 12 Utopian Years, the equivalent of 12 weeks in Earth time.

Currency

The currency of choice in the Utopian World is the Gold Coin, noted by "gc". Unfortunately, the peasants of Utopia have no sense of the Dollar, Pound, Franc, or other Earth currencies that you may be familiar with.

Races

The population of Utopia is made up of a mix of nine races. I advise you to pick your race carefully. Consider also your Kingdom, as they will be an important part of your experience in Utopia. Many Kingdoms choose to make their race and personality choices as a team, and finding the right mix can be an important part of developing your Kingdom strategy.

For specific details on both Races and Personalities for the current Age of Utopia, please click here!

  • Avians are a birdlike race with wings that travel lightning fast. As Avians prefer to attack from the air, their military do not use warhorses. Their forces strike with precision and accuracy from the air and disappear so quickly that it is difficult for opposing armies to ambush them. These provinces are best suited to be guided by rulers who can keep up with their speed!
  • Dwarves are highly adaptable. Depending on the inclinations of those guiding them, they are capable of attacking, magic and thievery, and under the right rule, they can be great economists. Their sturdy and tough armies can make them a force to be reckoned with. Dwarves are also a very hard-working race, with an above-average level of productivity and a particular talent for construction.
  • Faeries are a quick, nimble and intelligent race, excelling at defending their lands. They are mischievous creatures, thriving on causing chaos - particularly via the methods of magic and thievery.
  • Humans are proficient in the scientific arts, always seeking to further their knowledge. This unique strength allows them to develop advanced skills in whichever areas they choose.
  • Orcs are powerful, bloodthirsty attackers. Their aggressive approach yields them great gains in battle, however, their lack of finesse means the arts of magic and thievery present great challenges for them
  • The Undead are formidable opponents in battle, however their low intelligence hampers their prowess in scholarly endeavours, and their powerful elite units can only be trained through combat. They are carriers of the Plague, and those who encounter them on the battlefield risk infection with this devastating disease.
  • Halflings are talented saboteurs. Although their small stature means their troops are not especially powerful, it allows them to house large populations, and their armies are equally comfortable attacking and defending. These cunning creatures are not to be underestimated!
  • Gnomes are the most mysterious of the races, the Gnomes are short legged individuals who live in natural areas close to the Earth and care for wildlife. Gnomes are versatile and will be a good pick for players who want to focus on combining Attacking with Thievery or Magic.

Personalities

As well as a race, you will also choose a personality. Utopian rulers have nine different personalities to choose from. Although there is no ‘best’ combination, new rulers who are just starting out are advised to pick a race and personality that share similar core strengths to build upon - for example, if you have chosen an attacking-oriented race, such as Orc, you might consider pairing it with a similarly oriented personality, such as Warrior.

  • The Rogue is a master thief, well trained in the arts of stealing and sabotage. Operating in the darkest of shadows, the Rogue is able to perform dastardly acts others can scarcely imagine.
  • The Mystic is a powerful mage, with the ability to visit devastating destruction upon their enemies through spellcasting.
  • The Cleric is a noble and resilient fighter. The Cleric is especially gifted at supporting others, with the ability to cast unique spells on their allies.
  • The Warrior wields a great strength on the battlefield, preferring an aggressive approach to overpowering their enemies.
  • The Tactician is a strategic mastermind, targeting their enemies’ weaknesses with deadly cunning and precision. The Tactician is renowned for their speed on the battlefield.
  • The Heretic adheres to an unorthodox combination of thievery and magic, combining both fields into unique talents of damage and destruction. Although they can sometimes find their more specialised counterparts to present a challenge, adept Heretic rulers can still find greatness.
  • The War Hero is known for their great deeds on the battlefield, and for their firm belief that with risks come great rewards. They fight valiantly and reap rewards such as honor and promotions to elite ranks.
  • The Artisan is a swift and resourceful builder, as well as a careful manager of economy.
  • The Raider combines thievery and ruthlessness to convert captured enemies into their own ranks.

Commonly used Acronyms and Definitions

Utopians communicate mainly in English, however there are a few new terms you may not yet be familiar with. Here are a few of the most commonly used Utopian terms you will encounter:

  • KD = Kingdom
  • BE = Building Efficiency
  • ME = Military Efficiency
  • OME = Offensive Military Efficiency
  • DME = Defensive Military Efficiency
  • OPA = Offense Per Acre
  • OSPA = Offense Specialists Per Acre
  • DPA = Defense Per Acre
  • DSPA = Defense Specialists Per Acre
  • EPA = Elites Per Acre
  • TPA = Thieves Per Acre
  • RTPA = Raw Thieves Per Acre
  • MTPA = Modified Thieves Per Acre
  • WPA = Wizards Per Acre
  • RWPA = Raw Wizards Per Acre
  • MWPA = Modified Wizards Per Acre
  • PPA = Peasants Per Acre
  • TM = Traditional March
  • T/M = Thief/Mage
  • FB = Fire Ball
  • LL = Land Lust
  • Dice = Paradise
  • MV = Mystic Vortex
  • Prop = Propaganda
  • MP = Minor Protection
  • Prot = Protection
  • WW = War Win
  • Crown = Winning Chart
  • EOA = End of Age
  • CF = Cease-Fire
  • EOWCF = End Of War Cease-Fire
  • Wave = Large group of co-ordinated attacks
  • OOP = Out Of Protection
  • Overpop = Generally used when your total population is so far over your maximum population that you can't attack
  • PK = Peasant Kill - this happens when you have 0 peasants, at which point your province will perish
  • Chain = Co-ordinated attacks against a single target in a specific order for maximum gains and damage
  • Bounce = A failed attack
  • UB = Unbreakable (referring to defense)
  • Bush = Short for Ambush
