Using your Thieves

Moving through the shadows, our thieves are an essential part of our kingdom operations. We can send our thieves to conduct espionage against our enemies, or to perform acts of sabotage to disrupt their operations. The success rate of our operations depends on our thievery effectiveness relative to that of our enemy. Thievery effectiveness is primarily determined by the number of thieves we have in relation to the size of our lands. Factors that contribute to our overall thievery effectiveness may include racial and personality bonuses, our honour level, our investment in Crime science, and any Thieves’ Dens we have built. However – no matter how effective our thieves, they can also be thwarted by our enemies’ Watch Towers, or the Clear Sight spell. Our thieves rely on stealth to perform their dastardly deeds; each operation you send your thieves on will consume a small amount of stealth, which then recharges slowly over time. Your thieves do have a sense of self-preservation, and will refuse to perform operations if their stealth drops below 5%.

Thief Operations

Gathering intelligence on your enemies is a crucial part of warfare and our thieves have a variety of different operations to obtain this information. To ensure accurate results, you must send 10% of your thieves on each infiltration.

The reconnaissance missions available are:

  • Spy on Throne - Retrieve detailed information about the total resources and troops owned by your enemy.
  • Spy on Military - Your thieves infiltrate your opponent's armies, and inform you of the number of their troops at home, out on missions, and in training.
  • Spy on Defense - Retrieve a simple message containing the detials of your enemy's defense at home.
  • Survey - Sneak into your enemy's land and investigate their buildings. This displays the number and effect of completed buildings only.
  • Spy on Science - Spy on your enemy's researchers and discover the advancement of their studies.
  • Infiltrate - Penetrate the enemy's Thieves Guild and count their numbers.
  • Snatch News - Steal a newspaper from your enemy, allowing you to see the attacks performed by and against their Kingdom, as well as any rituals or dragon projects that they have started, any wars they have begun or ended, and information about provinces that have joined or departed the kingdom during the current and previous Utopian Month.

A diverse range of offensive thievery operations is available to damage our enemies, and the rogue personality offers specialized training for additional operations. Due to the aggressive nature of some operations, a minimum relationship to the enemy's kingdom is required. Learn about The Hostile Meter for more information regarding the relationships between Kingdoms.

The sabotage operations available to all provinces are:

  • Rob the Granaries - Steal a generous amount of food.
  • Rob the Vaults - Steal gold from the enemy's coffers.
  • Rob the Towers - Steal runes from your enemy's towers.
  • Kidnapping - Abduct a portion of the enemy's peasants and return some of these to our province.
  • Sabotage Wizards - Exhaust enemy wizards to lower their available mana.
  • Bribe Thieves - Reduce the enemy's thievery effectiveness for a time until they find the double agents.
  • Bribe Generals - Provides a chance for the enemy's military to incur greater losses until the turncoat is discovered and executed.
  • Arson - Burns down a small amount of enemy buildings, reverting the acres to barren lands.
  • Incite Riots - Disrupt tax collection for several days, decreasing the enemy's income.
  • Free Prisoners - Release a portion of the enemy's prisoners from their dungeons.
  • Night Strike - Requires a minimum relation of Unfriendly - Assassinates a portion of the enemy's military, regardless of whether they are at home or away on a mission.

Additionally, Rogues can access a number of powerful exclusive operations, these are:

  • Steal War Horses - Captures a portion of the enemy's horses, returning some of these to our province. Only horses that are not out on attack can be stolen.
  • Greater Arson - Requires a minimum relation of Unfriendly – Similar to Arson, but allows thieves to target a specific type of building. A portion of the selected buiding type is burnt and reverted to barren acres.
  • Assassinate Wizards - Requires a minimum relation of Unfriendly - A difficult operation that slaughters a portion of the enemy's wizards.
  • Destabilize Guilds - Decreases the target's duration of Self Spells and Offensive Spell casts while the effect is active by 20%.
  • Propaganda - Only available during War - Attempts to convince enemy soldiers, elites, specialists, thieves or wizards to revolt and join our ranks.
